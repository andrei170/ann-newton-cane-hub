Questions

The Ones I'd Expect You To Have

Short, direct answers. The longer ones we cover on the call.

Do I need an existing audience first?

No. The Sotheby's panels you've already spoken on, the LinkedIn audience you already reach, and the Women Empowered alumni list are the audience. The first mastermind sells off existing demand. A typical first mastermind for an operator at your tier sells 12–20 seats off cold-warm outreach to the existing network plus one round of paid acquisition. Audience-building runs in parallel, in the background, for Phase 2 and 3, but Phase 1 doesn't depend on it.

Does this conflict with my Sotheby's relationship?

No. You're already invited to teach for Sotheby's. This doesn't replace that, it sits alongside it. Your speaker rate at Sotheby's panels does not go down because you also have your own product. The audience for both is the same audience: peer agents. The difference is that here, you keep the asset, the email list, the recordings, and the follow-on revenue. We can also build the offer in a way that complements the brokerage's existing programs rather than competing with them.

How much time does this require from me?

One day of filming per month. Two days for the in-person mastermind. Bi-weekly mentorship calls in Phase 2. The rest is Francis's team handling production and Andrei's side handling funnel, copy, and ad work. Most operators at your stage spend less than 10 hours per month on the system once it's built. You don't become a content creator. You stay an agent who happens to also have a high-ticket teaching business.

Does this conflict with my brokerage business?

No. The mastermind and mentorship audiences are other agents, not your buyers and sellers. Zero competitive overlap with the listings business. If anything, the public-facing visibility lifts the brokerage too, because every asset reinforces your numbers and your specialty. Several agents who've built systems like this have actually seen meaningful lift to their listing business, because the public-facing teaching content acts as a credibility halo for prospective sellers and buyers as well.

How quickly is there real revenue?

First mastermind: 30–45 days from kickoff. First mentorship cohort: month two onward. Webinar and course revenue: month three onward, ramping into month six. The accelerator is the in-person event, because seats sell faster off a fixed date than an open enrollment. Phase 2 community converts off the mastermind room, which is why Phase 1 is the wedge.

What about December through February when the market goes quiet?

This is the entire reason to build it. Real estate slows. Info offers don't. The mentorship cohort that starts in November pays in advance and runs through Q1. The mastermind for spring is pre-sold in December and January. The webinar funnel runs continuously. Year-round revenue is the unlock, and it lands in your account during the months your peers are watching the calendar.

Why not just build YouTube and Instagram first?

Audience-building from scratch is a 12-to-24-month exercise. High-ticket sells off existing network in days. Phase 1 doesn't need a million followers. It needs the right twenty seats sold to peer agents already in your contact list and on the panels you've already spoken at. Phase 3 benefits from social, and we build it in the background while Phase 1 and 2 produce revenue. By month six the social and YouTube presence is mature enough to drive a meaningful share of cohort enrollment. By year two it becomes the primary acquisition channel.
